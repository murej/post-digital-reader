    • #11

      In this permanent flow of increasing information and momentary experiences, collection and re-contextualisation seems to be the only way of providing meaning. However, if we completely abolish ownership, how will society value information, how can we measure the value of perceived meaning and what will motivate people to produce? We can look at it from the angle of marking sustainable information as valuable information. Sustainability of material media is achieved through their archival value and permanence, whereas sustainable digital products guarantee permanent flow of unique experiences. In order to achieve them, they require constant activity, either through algorithmic or user participation. The first is automatic, yet artificial, while the latter requires a motivated and engaged user, who needs to see value in what he gets in return, which is a product of perception as aesthetic experiences. Increase in information value therefore demands aesthetic experiences.

    • #12

      Murray argues that “the appropriate design goal for interactive environments is not the degree of interactivity, but whether or not the system creates the satisfying experience of agency for the interactor (...) [to] increase rather than obstruct the agency of the interactor”, essentially appealing to create transparent technology, which efficiently solves problems. While this is clearly an advancement in making technology humane, it should also strive to make technology colourful. “User-friendliness helps naturalize electronic objects and the values they embody. (...) their use is constrained by the simple generalized model of a user these objects are designed around (...) objects, it seems, must be understood, rather than interpreted.” In order to encourage curiosity, we need to enable users to interpret and perceive it in new and meaningful ways.
